Who Shouldn't Use Medical Weight Loss Treatment

For your safety, medical weight loss medications aren't suitable for everyone. You won't be eligible if you:

Are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning pregnancy

Have personal or family history of thyroid cancer (MTC or MEN2)

Have severe kidney disease or end-stage renal disease

Have a history of pancreatitis

Are under 18 years old

Have active eating disorders

If you have any of these conditions, our prescribers will discuss alternative approaches during your consultation.

Not sure? Complete the assessment anyway — our clinical team will review your full medical history and advise on the best approach for you.

Eligibility Questions Answered

You typically need a BMI of 30 or above to qualify. However, if you have a BMI of 27 or above AND have a weight-related health condition (such as type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, high cholesterol, sleep apnoea, PCOS, or joint problems), you may also be eligible. Our prescribers will assess your individual circumstances during the clinical review.
Yes, you may be eligible. If you have a BMI of 27 or above with a weight-related health condition like high blood pressure, you meet the clinical criteria for medical weight loss treatment. Complete our free assessment and our prescribers will review your specific situation to confirm eligibility.
No, you don't need a GP referral. Our service includes a full clinical assessment by UK-registered independent prescribers who can prescribe treatment directly. However, we do recommend informing your GP that you're starting treatment so they can update your medical records and provide coordinated care.
If you don't meet the criteria for prescription weight loss medication, our clinical team will explain why and discuss alternative approaches. This might include lifestyle modification support, nutritional guidance, or other evidence-based weight management strategies. We're here to help you find the right solution for your situation.
These medications are not currently licensed for use in people with Type 1 diabetes. They are approved for Type 2 diabetes and weight management. If you have Type 1 diabetes and are interested in weight loss support, please discuss this with your diabetes specialist team who can recommend appropriate options for your specific needs.
Taking other medications doesn't automatically disqualify you, but it's important to disclose all medications during your assessment. Our prescribers will review potential interactions and determine if treatment is safe for you. Some medications may require dose adjustments or additional monitoring, which our team will manage with you.
You must be 18 or over to qualify for treatment. There's no upper age limit, but our prescribers will consider your overall health, any age-related conditions, and whether treatment is appropriate and safe for you. Many of our successful patients are in their 50s, 60s, and beyond.
Previous experience with weight loss medications doesn't disqualify you. In fact, it's helpful information for our prescribers. Please share details about what you've tried before, how you responded, and any side effects you experienced. This helps us determine the most suitable treatment for you and set realistic expectations.
There's no specific time requirement for how long you've been overweight. The key criteria are your current BMI and whether you've struggled to lose weight through diet and exercise alone. Our prescribers focus on your current health status and weight loss goals rather than the duration of being overweight.
Previous weight loss surgery doesn't automatically disqualify you, but it requires careful assessment. Some people regain weight after bariatric surgery and may benefit from medication support. Our prescribers will review your surgical history, current anatomy, and whether medication is appropriate and safe in your specific case. Please provide full details during your assessment.
